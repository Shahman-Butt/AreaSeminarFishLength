Fish Length Estimation with Vision Foundation Models:
Can They Beat the AutoFish CNN Baseline?

Shahman Butt
Area Seminar — Deep Learning for Maritime Vision Applications, Visual and Analytic Computing in Ocean Technologies (VACOT), University of Rostock  ·  Supervisor: Stefan Oehmcke

Motivation & Question

  • Fish length is a key measurement for fisheries science and catch monitoring — manual measuring is slow and invasive.
  • The AutoFish benchmark (Bengtson et al., WACVW 2025) provides images, annotations, and a CNN length-regression baseline.
  • Vision foundation models promise strong general features — but do they help precise metric regression?
Question: Do foundation-model encoders (DINOv2, CLIP) or newer supervised encoders (ConvNeXt) improve fish length estimation over the reproduced AutoFish MobileNetV2 baseline — under identical data, splits, and metrics?

Data & Setup

  • AutoFish dataset: 1,500 top-view images, 454 unique fish, 18,157 fish annotations, organized in 25 fish groups.
  • Official group-level split: 15 train / 5 validation / 5 test groups — no fish appears in two splits (one duplicate fish identity found and removed).
  • Two test regimes: non-occluded (Set1+Set2, fish laid separately) vs. occluded (“All”, fish overlapping).
  • Metric: mean absolute error (MAE) of predicted length in cm — lower is better. RMSE, MAPE, and R² tracked as secondary metrics.

Key findings

  • Baseline reproduced within 0.013 cm of the paper on non-occluded fish.
  • MobileNetV2 remains best overall: 0.771 cm full-test MAE.
  • Foundation features are not automatically better. Frozen DINOv2 reaches only 1.738 cm; even its best variant (last-block fine-tuned) stays at 1.439 cm.
  • CLIP transfers far better than DINOv2 (0.958 cm with light fine-tuning) but still does not beat the CNN.
  • Supervised ImageNet encoders are the closest challengers: ConvNeXt-Tiny reaches 0.914 cm.
  • Occlusion hurts every model: e.g. the baseline degrades from 0.633 cm (separate fish) to 0.909 cm (overlapping fish).

Take-home message

For precise fish length regression, a small task-adapted CNN still beats vision foundation models. Strong general features are no substitute for end-to-end task adaptation when millimetres matter.

Limitations

  • One training run per model (fixed seed) — differences of a few hundredths of a cm are not yet statistically validated.
  • Foundation models tested at small scale (ViT-S/14, ViT-B/32) with limited fine-tuning budgets — larger variants might behave differently.
  • No per-species or per-length-range error analysis yet.

Next steps

  • EfficientNet-B0 as the next supervised-encoder candidate.
  • Repeat the top models with 3 seeds to quantify run-to-run variance.
  • Error analysis by species, fish size, and occlusion difficulty.
